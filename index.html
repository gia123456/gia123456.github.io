What Where the Australian Wildfires?

Each year, Australians face a treacherous season of summer fires, when humidity and blazing weather conditions make flames more contagious than the common cold. While many natural causes are to blame, scientists explain that human-made climate changes are largely responsible for the increased longevity of the fires that struck Australia between September of 2019 through February of 2020. Burning an estimated amount of 18.6 million hectares over the course of 210 days, the Australian wildfires resulted in the casualties of many people and animals. Professionals estimated that over 110 different species would need urgent assistance, considering the destruction of their habitats, and severe injuries sustained from the flames.
